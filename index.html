About

Sri. K. Sreenivasan passed away on 10 May 2026 at his daughter's residence in Bangalore, following nearly a year of illness. He was 85 years old. His mortal remains were brought to Ernakulam the following morning.

One of the tallest and most respected leaders of the bank retirees' movement in India, he served his colleagues — active and retired — with unwavering dedication for over five decades. His contribution to the cause of bank retirees was invaluable and will always be remembered with gratitude.

Positions held

▸General Secretary, All Kerala Bank Retirees Federation (AKBRF)
▸National Chairman, All India Bank Retirees Federation (AIBRF)
▸National Asst. Secretary, All India Bank Employees Association (AIBEA)
▸General Secretary, All Kerala Bank Employees Federation (AKBEF)
▸President, SBT Employees Union

A life of service

He began his union career as an employee of the State Bank of Travancore (SBT), rising through the ranks to lead the bank employees' movement in Kerala. As AIBEA Assistant Secretary, he participated in numerous bilateral negotiations with the Indian Banks' Association. He was among the signatories to the landmark 7th Bipartite Settlement, alongside Comrades N. Sampath and Tarakeshwar Chakravarti.

He played a key role in agitations at Gramin Bank, Federal Bank, KDCC Bank, and Nedungadi Bank. His contributions in introducing pension in commercial banks and computerising commercial banks remain historic milestones. He led the charge when bank employees fought to secure pension rights — and continued that fight as a retiree.

After retiring from SBT in 2001, he dedicated himself fully to the bank retirees' movement. His leadership of AKBRF became a model for other states. He organised private sector bank retirees under the AIBRF banner, built the AKBEF headquarters near the KSRTC Bus Stand in Ernakulam, and established the AKBRF state committee office at North Ernakulam Railway Station. During the COVID-19 pandemic, he kept the organisational leadership active through continuous online expert-led programmes — widely noticed at the national level.

Family

He is survived by his wife Omana, son Anoop (US), daughter Anooja (Bangalore), daughter-in-law Brinda (US), and son-in-law Bibin (Bangalore).

Official condolence — AIBRF

All India Bank Retirees' Federation (Regd.) · Ref No. 2026/0161 · 10 May 2026

It is with profound grief and deep sorrow that we inform you of the sad demise of Shri K. Sreenivasan, former Chairman of AIBRF and General Secretary of the All Kerala Bank Retirees' Federation (AKBRF), who passed away on 10th May 2026, in Bangalore, at the age of 85 years.

Shri K. Sreenivasan was one of the tallest and most respected leaders of the bank retirees' movement in India. He commanded immense respect not only among bank retirees in Kerala but throughout the country. His contribution to the cause of bank retirees was invaluable and will always be remembered with gratitude.

He also served with distinction as General Secretary of the All Kerala Bank Employees' Union for several years and played a pivotal role in strengthening the trade union movement.

The passing away of Shri Sreenivasan is a great and irreparable loss to AIBRF and to the entire bank retirees' movement in India. His dedication, leadership, vision, and commitment to the welfare of retirees will continue to inspire us for years to come.

The entire AIBRF family mourns his demise and conveys its heartfelt condolences to the bereaved family members. We pray to the Almighty to grant eternal peace to the departed soul and strength and courage to the family to bear this irreparable loss.

Om Shanti.

— S. C. Jain, General Secretary, All India Bank Retirees' Federation

Personal tribute

I did not know Sreenivasan Sir before joining Indian Overseas Bank. When I received the SBT interview call, my father — an early CPI worker — told me: "Go there, we have our people." With Sreenivasan Sir's assurance, I joined SBT, left IOB, and became active in the union. What followed was nearly thirty-five years of closeness.

I learned to speak and write from Sreenivasan Sir. Two poetry collections, a music album, a short-story collection — all carry his inspiration. Today, on hearing the news of his passing, I took up this pen as a feeble solace.

I do not say farewell, Comrade... Go be with my father. Continue your organisational work in that unseen world. We shall meet there soon.

— വിട പറയുന്നില്ല സഖാവേ, Jelin Kumbalamn
